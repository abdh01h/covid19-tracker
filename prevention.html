Wash Hands
Washing the hands with soap or hand sanitizer helps kill any viruses on the hands that people may have come into contact with. Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands.
Keep Your Distance
People should take care to avoid coming into close contact with others — especially those who are older, unwell, or have symptoms of the virus.
The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) recommend staying 6 feet away from anyone who is coughing or sneezing.
Wear Face Mask
COVID-19 spreads mainly from person to person through respiratory droplets. Respiratory droplets travel into the air when you cough, sneeze, talk, shout, or sing.
These droplets can then land in the mouths or noses of people who are near you or they may breathe these droplets in.

Masks are a simple barrier to help prevent your respiratory droplets from reaching others. Studies show that masks reduce the spray of droplets when worn over the nose and mouth.
Cover Coughs & Sneezes
If a person has mild symptoms of COVID-19, they can self-isolate by staying at home and avoiding contact with others.

Even if a person is unsure whether they have COVID-19, a common cold, or something else, it is best to stay inside and rest.
